Project Grand Totals Fractions Summed & Rounded Up
Why is the Plumbing Design Load higher? Per CPC Section 422.1.1, when dividing a room's base occupant load by sex, odd numbers must be rounded UP to the next whole number for both males and females. Because this calculator evaluates your project space-by-space, this mandatory per-room rounding often results in a Plumbing Design Load that is higher than your raw Base Occupants.
All-Gender Restroom Calculations: Per 2025 CPC Section 422.1.1, the minimum required fixture count for all-gender facilities is the aggregate (sum) of the Male and Female fractional calculations. Important: A single provided all-gender fixture can be counted toward either a required male fixture or a required female fixture, but not both simultaneously. The total combined number of fixtures provided across the project must always meet or exceed the total aggregate required.
